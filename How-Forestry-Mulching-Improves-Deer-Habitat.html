How Forestry Mulching Improves Deer Habitat

Strategic forestry mulching can dramatically improve deer habitat by creating bedding cover, encouraging browse growth, and improving travel corridors across hunting properties.

Request a Free Estimate

Why Habitat Management Matters

Many rural properties in Wisconsin contain mature woods with very little sunlight reaching the forest floor. When that happens, the understory disappears and wildlife habitat becomes poor.

Forestry mulching allows landowners to selectively remove brush, invasive species, and low-value trees while opening the canopy enough for sunlight to reach the ground. This encourages natural regeneration of native plants that provide food and cover for deer.

Key Habitat Improvements Forestry Mulching Can Create

🦌 Bedding Cover

Selective clearing encourages thick regeneration of young trees, grasses and shrubs that provide excellent bedding cover for whitetail deer.

🌿 Natural Browse

When sunlight reaches the ground, new growth of blackberry, raspberry, forbs and other browse plants appear — an important natural food source for deer.

🛻 Travel Corridors

Mulching can open trails and travel corridors that naturally guide deer movement across a property and improve hunting access.

Common Habitat Projects

Landowners often use forestry mulching for projects such as:

  • Opening travel corridors between bedding and feeding areas
  • Creating small food plot openings
  • Improving edge habitat
  • Removing invasive species like buckthorn
  • Clearing access trails for hunting and property management

These improvements help create a healthier and more diverse habitat while making the property easier to manage long term.
