Habitat Improvement

Selective clearing and forestry mulching can improve wildlife habitat by allowing sunlight to reach the forest floor. That can help native grasses, forbs and browse plants grow again, which improves food and cover for deer and other wildlife.

Many landowners use forestry mulching to open hunting property, improve bedding cover, create travel corridors, widen UTV trails and prepare access to future food plots while making the woods healthier overall.
