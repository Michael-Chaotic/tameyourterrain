How Forestry Mulching Improves Deer Habitat

Overgrown woods often block sunlight from reaching the forest floor. Selective clearing and forestry mulching can open portions of the woods back up so desirable vegetation can grow again.

This type of work can improve food sources, cover and movement for deer, turkey and other wildlife. It can also make your property easier to access and manage.

Forestry mulching is also useful for preparing future food plot areas and improving access to them. Clearing brush, widening UTV trails and opening small pockets of sunlight can make a property easier to manage.

No habitat work can guarantee bigger bucks, but better habitat can create conditions that encourage more consistent deer use, healthier browse and better cover.
