How Forestry Mulching Improves Deer Habitat

Strategic forestry mulching can help improve deer habitat by creating bedding cover, encouraging browse growth, and improving travel corridors across hunting properties.

Why Habitat Management Matters

Many rural properties in Wisconsin contain mature woods with very little sunlight reaching the forest floor. When that happens, the understory disappears and wildlife habitat becomes less productive.

Forestry mulching allows landowners to selectively remove brush, invasive species, and low-value trees while opening the canopy enough for sunlight to reach the ground. This can encourage natural regeneration of native plants that provide food and cover for deer.

Key Habitat Improvements Forestry Mulching Can Create

🦌 Bedding Cover

Selective clearing can encourage thick regeneration of young trees, grasses, and shrubs that provide better bedding cover for whitetail deer.

🌿 Natural Browse

When sunlight reaches the ground, new growth of blackberry, raspberry, forbs, and other browse plants can appear, helping improve natural food sources.

🛻 Travel Corridors

Mulching can open trails and travel corridors that help guide deer movement across a property while also improving hunting and management access.

Common Habitat Projects

Landowners often use forestry mulching for projects such as:

  • Opening travel corridors between bedding and feeding areas
  • Creating small food plot openings
  • Improving edge habitat
  • Removing invasive species like buckthorn
  • Clearing access trails for hunting and property management

These improvements can help create more diverse habitat while making the property easier to manage long term.
