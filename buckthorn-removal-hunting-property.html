How to Remove Buckthorn from Hunting Property

Buckthorn is one of the most aggressive invasive plants in Wisconsin and Minnesota. Removing it can dramatically improve woods, wildlife habitat and overall land health.

Why Buckthorn Is a Problem

Common buckthorn spreads aggressively through woodlots, fence lines and hunting properties. Once established, it forms dense thickets that block sunlight from reaching the forest floor.

This prevents native plants, grasses and tree seedlings from growing, which reduces biodiversity and degrades wildlife habitat over time.

For many landowners, buckthorn eventually takes over large sections of woods and makes the property difficult to walk, hunt or manage.

Benefits of Removing Buckthorn

🌿 Restore Native Growth

Once buckthorn is removed, sunlight can reach the forest floor again and native plants have a chance to return.

🦌 Improve Wildlife Habitat

Removing buckthorn allows browse, grasses and shrubs to grow, which improves food and cover for deer and other wildlife.

🌳 Healthier Woodlots

Clearing invasive brush helps trees regenerate and improves the long-term health of woodland properties.

How Forestry Mulching Helps Remove Buckthorn

Forestry mulching is one of the most efficient ways to clear heavy buckthorn infestations, especially across larger wooded areas.

Instead of cutting brush and leaving piles that must be burned or hauled away, a mulching machine processes the vegetation into small wood chips that remain on the ground.

This helps clear the area while leaving behind a protective mulch layer that reduces erosion and improves soil conditions.
